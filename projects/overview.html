Synthetic Data Generation with CARLA


Training machine learning models with the power of Unreal Engine

Today's modern artificial intelligence systems require a lot of data to train on. As Brunswick develops its autonomous boating capabilities, we find ourselves in need of more training data. This data is often hard to collect, as many of the situations we want to test for may be too dangerous for human drivers, or too rare to test too often. I explored CARLA as a potential solution to this problem. It's an open-source plugin for Unreal originally intended for training autonomous cars and other land vehicles. I adapted the system for a marine environment. With this modified version, we could control boat traffic, the weather, time of day, the environment, and even more, while recorded simulated sensor data like RGB cameras, stereocameras, and LiDAR sensors.

Block On


Procedural voxelization in Blender

Blender's native voxelization is clunky. It's a slow and destructive process, one that I wanted to improve. I created a Python add-on that allows artists to quickly voxelize their 3D models without needing to retexture their models. The plugin gives the artist more control and to find new ways of expressing ideas in the style. With Geometry Nodes, the plugin is completely procedural, allowing for quick variations and expansions.

NodeToPython


Converting Geometry Node groups to add-ons

Blender's procedural geometry system is an incredible tool, but distributing node setups isn't the easiest process. Previously, one would have to share the entire project file just to share these node groups. I coded a Blender add-on that analyzes Geometry Node networks and creates a Python script to replicate it. Artists and add-on creators can now contribute to Blender's ecosystem and easily share their work without needing to know how to code.
